TEMPLATE 17: BURNOUT (HONEST BUT PROFESSIONAL)

Subject: Resignation – [Your Name]

Dear [Manager Name],

I am resigning from my position as [Job Title] at [Company Name], effective [Last Day].

After [X years/months] of intense work, I have reached a point where I need to step back and prioritize my well-being. I recognize that I am no longer able to perform at the level this role demands, and it's time for a reset.

I am grateful for [specific positives: working on [project], collaborating with [team], learning [skill]]. I will ensure [transition tasks] are completed to the best of my ability before my departure.

Thank you for your understanding.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]

---
NOTES:
- Burnout is REAL and valid (don't apologize for it)
- Frame as "prioritizing well-being" (positive framing, not victimhood)
- If pressed for details, redirect: "I've consulted with my doctor, and taking time off is the right call."

HOW TO USE:
1. Replace [placeholders]
2. Be honest but professional
3. No need to over-explain
4. Set firm boundary if manager pushes back
5. Take care of yourself first
